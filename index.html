Kolink Rocket v4

Completed in 2019, this is my eighth build to date. It was my first M-ITX, and involved custom modifications based on

  • Kolink Rocket case M-ITX case
  • Aorus B450 Pro Wifi ITX
  • AMD Ryzen 5 3600
  • Asetek 645LT 92mm AIO
  • 16gb Vulcan T-Force 3000mhz ddr4 RAM
  • Nvidia Geforce 1080ti FE
  • 1.5tb Silicon Power NVME m2 SSD
  • Corsair SF600 PSU (with included sleeved cables)
  • 1 x 92mm, 2 x 120m BeQuiet fans, Deepcool 4 hub fan controller

Custom handles

At 12.5 x 32.5 x 32.8cm, 9.6 litre volume (shoebox size) and 5kg this case is travel ready. The added handles only increase portability and make it an attractive option for those who travel and need their SFF to accompany them.

Custom legs & side panels

Legs and tinted acrylic plexi side panels added to hide the exhaust fans, improve breathability of the case and provide a more stealthy look.

Custom fans

Exhaust options were limited in the orignal design so 80mm holes were drilled to the underside of the case to accomodate two 80mm BeQuiet! Pure wings fans.

Solid construction

The titanium-grey anodised aluminium panels and minimalist design is impressive. The 2mm thick panels ensure your case can handle the day to day wear and tear it may encounter. Perforated holes on the GPU and CPU side mean coolers can constantly take in cool air.

Sandwich style layout

This smart and optimised layout is the most efficient use of space and allows the case to support up to 31cm dual slot GPUS, mini-ITX motherboards, SFX/SFX-L PSUs and 2 x 2.5" SSDs
